Q(1): OK, adding a note will take a few minutes depending on its content. Type your case name into the search bar at the top of Clio, and hit enter. This will take you to your case matter dashboard, which will look like this: 
<br><br><a href="https://suffolklitlab.org/clio/images/MatterDashboard.PNG" target="_blank"><img src="https://suffolklitlab.org/clio/images/MatterDashboard.PNG" style="width:232px;height:125px;"//></a><br><br>
Great. From here, click the "Notes" tab near the top of the page. Here, you can edit, add, or view notes from previous students and/or faculty that have worked on this case.
<br><br>Click on the blue "New note" button near the top right of the screen, below the tabs.
<br><br><a href="https://suffolklitlab.org/clio/images/newnote.png" target="_blank"><img src="https://suffolklitlab.org/clio/images/newnote.png" style="width:232px;height:62px;"/></a><br><br>
A popup will appear where you can add the subject, note, and date.
<br><br><a href="https://suffolklitlab.org/clio/images/noteentry.png" target="_blank"><img src="https://suffolklitlab.org/clio/images/noteentry.png" style="width:232px;height:198px"/></a><br><br>
The Date will always default to today's date, but can be changed by clicking on it. Make sure to add a subject that is a brief synopsis of what your note will be about (e.g., client meeting, client phone call, etc.)
<br><br>
Once you've added a subject line, type the  body of your note.
<br><br>
Click the blue "Save note" button at the bottom of the popup. The popup will close, and your new note will show up on the "Notes" tab as a new entry. GOTO:help
